# GDOT Reference Tables

Tables from Georgia Department of Transportation specifications for asphalt paving operations.

---

## Table 2 — Application Rates for Emulsified Asphalt (Tack Coat)

**Applies to:** Anionic Emulsified Asphalt or Cationic Emulsified Asphalt (gal/yd²)

| Tack Use | Min (gal/yd²) | Max (gal/yd²) |
|----------|:---:|:---:|
| New AC Pavement to New AC Pavement or Thin Lift Leveling | 0.05 (0.23 L/m²) | 0.08 (0.36 L/m²) |
| New AC (≤25% RAP) to Aged Existing Pavement or Milled Surface | 0.06 (0.27 L/m²) | 0.10 (0.45 L/m²) |
| New AC (>25% RAP) to Aged Existing Pavement or Milled Surface | 0.08 (0.36 L/m²) | 0.12 (0.54 L/m²) |

**Notes:**
- Allow standard emulsion to break per manufacturer's recommendation
- Proceed with paving only after emulsion has cured to the satisfaction of the Engineer
- Do NOT use anionic/cationic emulsified asphalt under OGFC or PEM on interstates or limited access state routes

## Table 4 — Lift Thickness vs. Minimum Temperature

| Lift Thickness | Minimum Air Temperature |
|:---:|:---:|
| 1 in. (25 mm) or less | 55°F (13°C) |
| 1.1 to 2 in. (26–50 mm) | 45°F (8°C) |
| 2.1 to 3 in. (51–75 mm) | 40°F (4°C) |
| 3.1 to 4 in. (76–100 mm) | 35°F (2°C) |
| 4.1 to 8 in. (101–200 mm) | 32°F (0°C) and rising; base must not be frozen |
